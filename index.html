Biography

I am an Infectious Disease physician-scientist and Assistant Professor of Medicine (with a joint appointment in Pediatrics) at Columbia University Vagelos College of Physicians and Surgeons. I also serve as Assistant Medical Director of the NYC STI/HIV Prevention Training Center (NYC PTC).

My research focuses on HIV and sexually transmitted infection (STI) prevention and treatment, with particular emphasis on using bioinformatics and machine learning to improve disease surveillance and care delivery. Recent work includes studies on doxycycline post-exposure prophylaxis (doxy-PEP) for STI prevention, mpox epidemiology and treatment (including the STOMP trial), and digital health interventions to increase STI testing and PrEP uptake in underserved urban populations.

Research

Areas of active investigation

HIV & STI Prevention

Developing and evaluating interventions to expand PrEP, PEP, and doxy-PEP uptake in high-risk urban populations, with a focus on health equity and underserved communities.

Mpox

Clinical management, epidemiology, and treatment of mpox, including investigation of long-term sequelae and participation in the STOMP trial evaluating tecovirimat.

Bioinformatics & Machine Learning

Applying natural language processing and machine learning to electronic health records to identify individuals at risk for HIV and STIs, and to improve real-world surveillance.

Antimicrobial Resistance

Investigating the impact of doxy-PEP on antimicrobial resistance patterns in clinical and community settings through the Doxy4STICare study.

Digital Health

Designing and testing digital patient decision aids to increase STI testing, doxy-PEP uptake, and HIV care engagement in emergency department and outpatient settings.

Congenital Syphilis

Characterizing epidemiology, clinical outcomes, and prevention strategies for congenital syphilis in the modern era across diverse U.S. populations.
